The Project

Our team is developing a hands-free wheelchair to increase accessibility for people with muscle dysfunction. Our system uses an electroencephalogram (EEG) headset scanner to move a wheelchair using the operator's brainwaves.

Our design process includes:
  • Reading brainwave data to our system from the EEG scanner
  • Implement a machine learning algorithm to analyze brainwave data
  • Creating the electrical control system for driving wheelchair motors
  • Produce engineering documents and drawings for future reference
  • Website development for product and system showcase

Our project was developed under the supervision and guidance of Dr. Kin Fun Li, PhD, MBA, PEng, SMIEEE
In not being able to source a motorized wheelchair, we worked on a proof of concept with an RC car. Our EEG scanner was from NeuroSky.

The car and corresponding radio controller were disassembled and modified to work with our arduino system. The EEG scanner gave us data for eSense(attention, meditation), signal quality, EEG band powers (delta, theta, alpha, beta, gamma), and raw EEG wave samples at 512hz. We used the corresponding data for attentiveness, meditativeness and eye blinking as action triggers for our system.

We initially worked on developing a training algorithm to classify our data. After unsuccessful attempts, we used the ThinkGear library by NeuroSky. The ThinkGear technology on the headset is what calculates the raw brainwave and eSense values for output. At publishing time we were able to successfully control forward moving and stopping actions through mind control.

Demo Day

We presented our proof of concept on July 27 2017 in the ELW building at the University of Victoria. Our demonstration was very successful, with many onlookers getting the opportunity to try our system and move the RC car with their brains. See our informational poster here.
